Manti

Description

Manti is a type of dumpling mainly found in Turkish cuisine, Armenian cuisine and Central Asian cuisine but also in West Asia, South Caucasus, and the Balkans. The dumplings typically consist of a spiced meat mixture, usually lamb or ground beef, wrapped in a thin dough sheet which is then boiled or steamed. The size and shape of manti vary significantly depending on geographic location. (Wikipedia)

Ingredients (For 4 servings)

    Dough:

  • 8 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons water, or as needed

    Filling:

  • 8 onions, peeled
  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • salt and pepper to taste

    Garlic Yogurt:

  • 4 (8 ounce) container plain yogurt
  • 4 tablespoons minced garlic

    Spiced Oil:

  • Turkish double concentrated tomato paste or red pepper paste (Biber Salçası):
  • Olive oil
  • Ground sumac
  • Red Pepper Flakes or Aleppo Pepper (Pul Biber)

    Optional Garnishes

  • Fresh or dried mint
  • Red Pepper Flakes
  • Whatever your heart desires!

These ingredients can vary slightly depending on the recipe

Directions

  1. Make dough: Combine flour and salt in a large bowl. Add eggs and water, mixing well with your hands. Add more water, if needed, to form a soft dough. Cover and set aside for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Make filling: Shred onions and place them in a colander or sieve set over a bowl; drain and discard juice. Combine drained onion, ground beef, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl; mix well with a spoon until mashed. Set filling aside.
  3. Lightly flour a work surface and a large plate.
  4. Divide dough in half. Working with one piece at a time, roll dough on the floured surface into a rectangle as thin as possible. Cut into 2-inch squares using a knife or pastry wheel.
  5. Place about 2 teaspoons filling in the center of each square. Gather the edges of dough and pinch them together at the top to form a bundle. Transfer manti to the prepared plate; sprinkle more flour on top to prevent sticking.
  6. Make oil: Heat oil and paste in a small skillet over low heat just until the ingredients begin to color oil. Remove from heat and keep warm.
  7. Make garlic yogurt: Stir together yogurt and garlic in a small bowl, add salt if needed; set aside.
  8. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over medium-high heat. Cook manti in boiling water until filling is no longer pink and dough is tender, 20 to 25 minutes. Drain well.
  9. After your manti is done cooking and plated, spoon yogurt over manti and drizzle with spiced oil. Add garnishes as you wish!
